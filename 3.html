Petroleum geology

Petroleum geology is the study of origin, occurrence, movement, accumulation, and exploration of hydrocarbon fuels. It refers to the specific set of geological disciplines that are applied to the search for hydrocarbons (oil exploration).

Geology is an earth science concerned with the solid Earth, the rocks of which it is composed, and the processes by which they change over time.


Geology can also include the study of the solid features of any terrestrial planet or natural satellite such as Mars or the Moon.

Modern geology significantly overlaps all other earth sciences, including hydrology and the atmospheric sciences, and so is treated as one major aspect of integrated earth system science and planetary science.
